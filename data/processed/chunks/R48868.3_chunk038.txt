Where Congress has expressly or implicitly authorized the President’s action, presidential power is at its “maximum” and “supported by the strongest of presumptions and the widest latitude of judicial interpretation.”175 Zone of Twilight . Cases in which Congress has neither authorized nor prohibited executive branch action lie in an inscrutable “zone of twilight in which [the President] and Congress may have concurrent authority, or in which its distribution is uncertain,” and “any actual test of power is likely to depend on the imperatives of events and contemporary imponderables rather than on abstract theories of law.”176 Lowest Ebb . Where Congress has expressly or impliedly prohibited executive action, presidential power “is at its lowest ebb,” as it is an assertion of executive authority “at once so conclusive and preclusive [that it] must be scrutinized with caution, for what is at stake is the equilibrium established by our constitutional system.”177 In the 1981 case Dames & Moore v.
