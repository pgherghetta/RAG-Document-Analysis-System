The Prohibitions on Private Inurement & Benefit by Tax -Exempt Organ izations and Intermediate Sanctions Congressional Research Service 5 regulations contain limits as to the types of private benefits prohibited.35 Thus, at least textually, more types of activities are prohibited under the prohibition on private benefit than under the prohibition on private inurement. However, because the prohibition on private inurement is applied more broadly than to just “net earnings,” the types of activities prohibited by the prohibition on private inurement and the prohibit ion on private benefit can overlap. Types of activities that may be prohibited private inurement and/or benefit include • loans extended on advantageous terms ,36 • partisan political activity ,37 • paying excessive rent38 or charging rent at less than fair marke t value ,39 • personal use of an organization ’s assets ,40 and • unreasonable compensation .41 Intermediate Sanctions In addition to the prohibitions on private inurement and private benefit, the I.R.C. contains excise taxes on certain transactions that can result in personal gain. This report explains the operation of three such excise taxes —those imposed by §§ 4941, 4958, and 4960.42 This report discusses how these three excise taxes differ in who is taxed, which transactions are taxed, how the tax is calculated, and the types of organizations subject to the tax. These excise taxes are intermediate sanctions short of revocation of tax exemption for violation of the prohibitions.
