In the case of compensation other than to a government official , the tax is paid only if the compensation is “excessive ,” that is , if the compensation exceeds the fair value of the services . In such a case, the “amount involved” is only the excess compensation.95 An initial, “first -tier” tax equal to 10% of the amount involved in the self -dealing transaction is imposed on the disqualified person .96 An additional tax of 200% of the amount involved is imposed on the disqualified person if the excess benefit transaction is not corrected within the taxable period.97 A foundation manager who knowingly98 participates in a self-dealing transaction is subject to a first-tier tax equal to 5% of the amount involved.99 The first -tier tax is not imposed on the manager if the participation is not “willful ” and is “due to reasonable cause. ”100 An additional tax of 50% of the amount involved is imposed on the foundation manager if the excess benefit transaction is not corrected within the taxable period and the manager refuse s to agree to the correction.101 The first-tier and additional taxes on managers are each capped at $20,000 for any one transaction.102 A foundation manager may be liable both as a foundation manager and as a disqualified person.103 T able 3.
