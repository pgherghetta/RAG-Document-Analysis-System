Basics of Fusion Energy Nuclear fusion is a process in which the nuclei of two lightweight atoms (such as hydrogen) join, or fuse, to form a heavier nucleus, releasing energy (see Figure 1). Fusion reactions take place in a hot, dense, ionized gas (i.e., one in which the electrons have separated) called plasma (the fourth state of matter) . Plasmas that generate a predefined ratio of energy from the ongoing nuclear fusion reactio n relative to the added external energy from the experimental apparatus are deemed burning plasmas .
