In March 2018, Congress , in the Making Opportunities for Broadband Investment and Limiting Excessive and Needless Obstacles to Wireless Act (MOBILE NOW Act ; P.L. 115 -141, Title VI, §605(b) ), directed the FCC to evaluate the feasibility of allowing commercial wireless use (e.g., 5G) in the C -band . In March 2020, the FCC adopted final rules for the C -band. The FCC relocated the incumbent satellite operators to the Upper 200-megahertz segment of this 500 -megahertz band (4.0 -4.2 GHz), freeing up the Lower C-band (3.7 -3.98 GHz) to auction for 5G use. The FCC preserved a segment between the Lower and Upper C-band (3.98 -4.0 GHz) to serve as a guard band, to prevent mobile services in the Lower C-band from interfering with satellite service s in the Upper C-band .
