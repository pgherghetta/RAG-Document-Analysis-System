Public Broadcasting: Background Information and Issues for Congress Congressional Research Service 11 Changes to the NGWS Grant Program for FY2025 On August 6, 2025 , FEMA released a new notice of funding opportunity (NOFO) for the NGWS Grant Program . The NOFO modifie d the previous purpose and funding structure of the program as implemented by the Biden Administratio n, which had centered the CPB and public broadcasting interests in broad accordance with congressional appropriations committee reporting language (see the “Public Broadcasting Support for Public Alerts and Warning s” section) .63 The 2025 NOFO indicated that FEMA would fund a wider array of public and private sector stakeholders and activities in the emergency alerting field, placing less emphasis on the role of established broadcasters and upgra des of broadcast station technologies .64 The 2025 NOFO appears to indicate that FEMA may exclude participation in the program by organizations that have diversity, equity, and inclusion practices and by foreign nationals .65 In contrast with previous funding opportunity notices, the 2025 NOFO made no mention of public broadcasting stations or public broadcasting audiences as intended beneficiaries of the grant program.66 The 2025 NOFO made $ 40 million available to fund improvements to emergency alerting systems to inform the public during emergencies and protect critical infrastructure .
