At the light end of the scale, deuterium and tritium are the furthest away from iron, so they have the greatest aspirations.”6 Achieving practical fusion energy requires three things: (1) heating a small quantity of fuel above its ignition point (about 100 million degrees Celsius), (2) maintain ing the reaction long enough for the release of fusion energy to exceed the total energy input, and (3) convert ing the ene rgy released to a useful form of energy (e.g. , electricity).7 To date, these three conditions have not reportedly been achieved.
