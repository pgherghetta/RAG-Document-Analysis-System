Ready to Learn Grants In recent decades , Ready to Learn (RTL) grants from the Department of Education have support ed the development of education al television and digital media targeted at preschool and early elementary school children.44 RTL grants are awarded on a competitive bas is every five years. The CPB was awarded grants in previous years to partner with local stations and other public broad casting entities that produce content for national distribution .45 In the past, RTL grants helped fund a number of well -known children’s programs, such as Sesame Street, Clifford the Big Red Dog, and Molly of Denali.46 In the 2020 -2025 grant cycle, the CPB was awarded a total of $112 million in order to develop “new content that helps young children build vital skills to help them succeed in school and life, including functional literacy, critical thinking and collaboration —and shows them career options in age -appropriate ways.”47 On March 20, 2025, the President issued Executive Order (E.O .) 14242 , “Improving Education Outcomes by Empowering Parents, States, and Communities.” The E.O. directs the Secretary of Education to “take all necessary ste ps to facilitate the closure of the Department of Education” while “ensuring the effective and uninterrupted delivery of services, programs, and benefits on which Americans rely.”48 On May 1, 2025 , the President issued E.O.
