Arms Control and Nonproliferation: A Catalog of Treaties and Agreements Congressional Research Service 20 The Outer Space Treaty The Treaty on Principles Governing the Activities of States in the Exploration and Use of Outer Space, including the Moon and Ot her Celestial Bodies (The Outer Space Treaty) entered into force in 1967. As of June 2025, there were 116 parties and 89 signatories to the treaty.101 Among other provisions, the treaty b ans the orbiting or stationing on celestial bodies (including the moon ) of nuclear weapons or other weapons of mass destruction. The Outer Space Treaty does not ban the placement of other weapons in outer space. The United States signed the treaty in January 1967 and deposited its instrument of ratification in October 1967. The Senate gave its advice and consent on April 25, 1967.102 The United States is one of three depositaries for the treaty. The 1997 UN General Assembly adopted a resolution (52/37) that aimed at Preventing an Arms Race in Outer Space, known as PAROS. The C onference on Disarmament subsequently reestablished an ad hoc committee to conduct negotiations related to the peaceful use of outer space. In coordination with the United Nations Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space (COPUOS), the United Nations C onference on Disarmament (CD) facilitates multilateral discussions on the peaceful use of outer space.
