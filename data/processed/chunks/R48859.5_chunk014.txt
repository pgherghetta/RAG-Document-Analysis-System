Mexico: Background and Key Issues in U.S. Relations Congressional Research Service 10 As part of its oversight of the U.S. -Mexico relationship and international trade, Congress may consider whether to whether to maintain, limit, or expand presidential authorities related to tariffs imposed under Section 122, IEEPA , or Section 232 that impact imports from Mexico. Congress may consider enacting legislation related to or otherwise indicating its views on these tariffs and their impact on relations with Mexico . Tariffs imposed under Section 122 expire after 150 days unless extended by an act of Congress. Congress also may consider whether tariffs imposed on Mexico under Section 122, IEEPA , and Section 232 are consistent with USMCA or other obligations under other international trade agreements.
