Congressional Proposals on the Intermediate Sanctions Congress has broad constitutional authority to select the “measure and objects ” of taxation and may use its taxing power to regulate private conduct.127 This authority includes the power to impose excise taxes, which are indirect tax es levied on specific activities.128 Congress also has broad authority to exempt certain things from taxation and to set the conditions for the exemption.129 Allegations of abuse of tax -exempt status by specific organizations has drawn congressional interest in the intermediate sanctions.130 Congress has proposed amendments that, if enacted, would change how the intermediate sanctions operate and to whom they apply. A current propo sal would expand the definition of “disqualified person” for the § 4958 excise tax.
