The Six Assurances in Legislation The varying verb tenses of individual assurances contribute to ambiguity about the degree to which they might be read as guiding future policy. Without resolving that ambiguity, since 2017, Congress has passed 11 laws with provisions referencing the Six Assurances , 9 of them National Defense Authorization Acts (NDAAs). The other laws are the Asia Reassurance Initiative Act of 2018 (P.L. 115 -409) and the Taiwan Assurance Act of 2020 ( P.L. 116 -260). • The NDAAs for FY2021 , FY2022, and FY2023 (P.L. 116-283, P.L. 117 -81, and P.L. 117 -263) state the sense of Congress that the TRA and the Six Assurances “are the foundation for” U.S.-Taiwan relations. The NDAAs for FY2019 and FY2020 ( P.L. 115 -232 and P.L. 116 - 92) state the sense of Congress that the TRA and the Six Assurances “are both cornerstones of ” U.S.-Taiwan ties. • The NDAA for FY2024 ( P.L. 118 -31) states the sense of Congress that “the United States’ one China policy, as guided by” the TRA, the three U.S. -PRC C ommuniqués , and the Six Assurances, “is the foundation for” U.S. - Taiwan relations. • The NDAAs for FY2022 through FY202 6 (including P.L. 118 -159 and P.L. 119 -60) state the sense of Congress that the United States should strengthen the U.S. partnership with Taiwan “consistent with ” the three joint c ommuniqu és, the TRA, and the Six Assurances. • The NDAA for FY20 18 (P.L. 115 -91) and P.L. 116 -260 state that it is U.S. policy to reinforce U.S. commitments to Taiwan “under” the TRA and “consistent with” the Six Assurances , with P.L. 116 -260 adding “in accordance with the United States ‘One China’ policy.” • P.L. 115 -409 states that it is U.S. policy “ to faithfully enforce all existing United States Government commitments to Taiwan, consis tent with [the TRA] , the 3 joint communiques, and the Six Assurances . In the 119th Congress, the Six Assurances to Taiwan Act (H.R. 3452 and S. 3208 ) would state that it is U.S. policy to reaffirm a version of the Six Assurances that combines the three existing official versions into one text. In that combined text, four assurances are in past tense, one include s both past and future tenses (the United States “did not and will not agree to play any mediation role”), and one is forward -looking . Addressing ambiguity about the effect of the Six Assurances , the act would provide for congressional review of specific proposed executive branch actions related to the Six Assurances : namely , actions “to pause or terminate” arms sales to Taiwan, negotiate with the PRC on Taiwan arms sales, mediate between Taiwan and the PRC over Taiwan’s sovereignty , change the lo ng- standing U.S. position on sovereignty over Taiwan, or pressure Taiwan to negotiate with the PRC.
